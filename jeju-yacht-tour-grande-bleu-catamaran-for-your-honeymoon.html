Aboard the Grande Bleu: What to Expect on Your Cruise

Your Jeju sailing adventure begins at Daepo Port in Seogwipo. Once aboard the spacious catamaran, you’ll embark on a 60-minute journey that showcases some of Jeju's most captivating coastal landmarks. Expect to glide past dramatic natural formations like Elephant Rock (Kkokkiribawi), the deep Jingotnae Sea Canyon, and the geologically significant Wolpyeong Basalt Columns. Keep an eye out for the mysterious Wolpyeong Sea Cave and the broad, photogenic Pangpaengideok reef plateau.

While enjoying the panoramic views, perhaps even catching a glimpse of wild dolphins, you won’t go hungry or thirsty. Grande Bleu takes onboard hospitality seriously with an incredible all-inclusive offering:

  • Free Unlimited Food & Drinks:
  • Draft beer (Korean fresh brew)
  • Wine (red & white)
  • Jeju tangerine juice (local fresh squeezed)
  • Bottled water
  • Jeju local snacks (cookies, traditional treats)
  • Korean ramyeon (instant noodles, cooked onboard)

Yes, that’s right – all free, unlimited refills, no extra charge. This makes the trip exceptionally good value, allowing you to simply relax and enjoy the moment without worrying about additional costs.

For a bit of interactive fun, a fishing experience is included on all tours. Fishing rods and fresh bait are provided free of charge, making it super easy for beginners. You might catch real Jeju fish like rockfish, scorpionfish, filefish, or even a cute pufferfish. There’s an option for catch-and-release, too, respecting the marine environment.

Try your hand at fishing with provided gear; a fun activity for couples on the Jeju yacht tour.

Sunset or Luxury: Picking Your Perfect Jeju Sailing Experience

Grande Bleu offers two primary types of tours: the Luxury Tour and the Sunset Tour. Both provide a fantastic opportunity for Jeju sailing, but cater to slightly different preferences.

The Luxury Tour runs throughout the day, typically at 11:00, 13:30, and 15:00. These daytime cruises are perfect for enjoying the vibrant blues of the ocean and the clear visibility of Jeju's unique rock formations.

However, for honeymooners visiting in spring (March-May), we highly recommend the Sunset Tour. This is where the magic truly happens. While times vary by season, in spring, the sunset tour usually departs around 16:30. This timing allows you to witness the 360-degree panoramic golden hour views as the sun dips below the horizon, painting the sky in breathtaking hues. It’s an incredibly romantic setting and offers unparalleled photo moments.
