Why a Catamaran, Not a Regular Yacht

Most "yacht tours" in Asia are monohull boats that lean and rock — fine if you have sea legs, less fine after a long-haul flight or if your partner gets queasy. A catamaran has two hulls, which means it sits flat on the water. You can walk around with a glass of wine without spilling it.

Grande Bleu is the only certified catamaran brand in Korea, which sounds like marketing until you actually compare it to the smaller fishing-style boats running out of other Jeju ports. The difference is obvious the moment you step on board.

For context: Shilla Hotel and Parnas Jeju both partner with Grande Bleu for guest experiences. Five-star hotels don't tend to attach their name to dodgy operators.

The Route: 60 Minutes of Volcanic Coastline

The boat leaves Daepo Port and follows Seogwipo's southern coast. This stretch is part of Jeju's UNESCO Global Geopark, and it's the kind of geology you don't really get to see from land.

In one hour you'll pass:

  • Elephant Rock (Kkokkiribawi) — a cliff that genuinely looks like an elephant drinking
  • Jingotnae Sea Canyon — vertical cliff walls that drop straight into the sea
  • Wolpyeong Basalt Columns — hexagonal volcanic pillars, similar to Northern Ireland's Giant's Causeway but with no entrance fee or queue
  • Wolpyeong Sea Cave and the Large Sea Cave (Keunbada Donggul)
  • Pangpaengideok — a flat reef platform that photographs well in golden hour light

In autumn (September to November), the sea stays calm and the light turns warm around 5–6 PM. October is the sweet spot — fewer summer crowds, mild temperatures, and sunsets that don't need a filter.

What's Actually Free Onboard

This is where the value tips in your favour. Everything on this list is unlimited and included in the ticket:

  • Draft Korean beer
  • Red and white wine
  • Freshly squeezed Jeju tangerine juice
  • Bottled water
  • Local Jeju snacks and cookies
  • Korean ramyeon, cooked onboard

The ramyeon sounds like a gimmick. It isn't. Eating instant noodles on a boat at sunset with a glass of wine is one of those small, slightly absurd pleasures that ends up being the part you remember.

For couples on a mid-range Jeju budget, this is where the maths starts working. A glass of wine in a Seogwipo hotel bar runs ₩15,000–20,000. Here it's bottomless.

The Fishing Bit (Yes, Really)

Rods, bait, and basic instruction are included. The boat slows down mid-route and anyone who wants to try gets a go. You might pull up rockfish, scorpionfish, filefish, or pufferfish — all genuinely local Jeju species.

It's catch-and-release if you prefer. No pressure, no extra cost. If fishing isn't your thing, the upper deck has the better view anyway.

Good to Know Before You Board

  • Wear flat, non-slip shoes. Heels and flip-flops are awkward on deck.
  • Arrive 20 minutes before departure (30 if travelling with seniors).
  • Bring a light windbreaker — sea-level temps run cooler than the port.
  • Restrooms exist onboard but they're tight. Use the port facilities first.
  • Weekday mornings are quietest if you want space.

Accessibility note: Daepo Port has short, mostly level access from the car park to the boarding point. Grande Bleu is also the only yacht in Jeju where life jackets are not required at boarding (the vessel meets the safety standard that exempts it), which makes the climb on board easier for older travellers or anyone with limited shoulder mobility.

FAQ

How long is the Jeju sunset cruise? The cruise runs about 60 minutes from departure to return at Daepo Port. The route covers Elephant Rock, the Wolpyeong basalt columns, and the sea caves along Seogwipo's southern coastline.

Is food included on the Jeju yacht? Yes. Unlimited draft beer, red and white wine, Jeju tangerine juice, bottled water, local snacks, and freshly cooked Korean ramyeon are all included at no extra cost.

Where does the Jeju yacht depart from? Grande Bleu departs from Daepo Port in Seogwipo, on the southern coast of Jeju Island (GPS 33.2287°N 126.4318°E). It's about a 20-minute drive from Jungmun Resort area.
