Why a Catamaran, and Why Bother?

Here's the honest bit. Most "yacht tours" in Asia are single-hull boats that pitch about the moment there's a swell. Nobody enjoys handing their kid a seasickness bag on a family holiday.

A catamaran sits on two hulls, so it stays flat. Grande Bleu is the only certified catamaran brand in Korea, and it's the only Jeju yacht rated safe enough to sail without mandatory life jackets on the upper deck. That matters if you're travelling with grandparents or fidgety kids who won't keep a vest on for an hour anyway.

Compared to the usual Bali or Greek island charter, you're getting the same photogenic-boat experience for roughly a third of the price. For backpacker budgets doing a family-vacation splurge, that's the appealing maths.

The 60-Minute Coastal Route

The boat leaves Daepo Port and hugs the south coast — a stretch of UNESCO-recognised volcanic geology you can't reach by road.

You'll pass:

  • Wolpyeong Basalt Columns — hexagonal sea cliffs, the same volcanic rock formation Jeju is famous for
  • Wolpyeong Sea Cave and the Large Sea Cave (Keunbada Donggul) — the biggest on the coast
  • Elephant Rock (Kkokkiribawi) — obvious once you see it from the water, invisible from land
  • Jingotnae Sea Canyon — dramatic vertical cliffs
  • Pangpaengideok reef plateau — a wide, flat lava shelf just above the waterline

If you time the sunset cruise right, the whole coast lights up gold. It happens once a day. You know the drill.

Fishing With the Kids

Rods, bait, and a crew member who'll actually help are all included. You're fishing for real Jeju species — rockfish, scorpionfish, filefish, the occasional puffer. Catch-and-release is fine if nobody in the group fancies gutting dinner.

It's genuinely one of the better Jeju family activities for kids aged five and up. They're occupied for twenty minutes, you get a photo, everyone wins.

Spring (March–May): Best Time to Go?

Yes, honestly. Jeju in spring sits around 15–20°C, the wind drops off, and you avoid both the summer typhoon window and the winter chop. May sunsets fall around 7:30pm, so the sunset cruise slots neatly after a lazy afternoon in Seogwipo.

The Jeju spring May trip window is also when Klook and other platforms tend to have lower load than July–August, so booking a few days ahead is usually enough.
