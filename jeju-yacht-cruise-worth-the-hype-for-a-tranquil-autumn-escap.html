What to Expect on Your Grande Bleu Jeju Sailing Adventure

The Grande Bleu yacht prides itself on offering a premium yet accessible experience. As Korea's only certified catamaran brand, they've hosted over 100,000 guests since 2011, even partnering with prestigious establishments like Shilla Hotel and Parnas Jeju. This isn't a fly-by-night operation; it's a trusted local operator.

The 60-minute sunset cruise is perfectly timed for an autumn evening in Jeju October, offering a peaceful atmosphere as the light softens. You'll glide past natural wonders like Elephant Rock (Kkokkiribawi), the Jingotnae Sea Canyon, and the UNESCO-recognized Wolpyeong Basalt Columns (Wolpyeong Jusangjeolli), an impressive geological formation.

You might even spot the elusive Wolpyeong Sea Cave or the vast Large Sea Cave (Keunbada Donggul). Keep an eye out for the adorable South Korean resident Indo-Pacific bottlenose dolphins – while not guaranteed, a sighting is a truly special bonus!

Reel in the Fun: Onboard Fishing in Jeju's Waters

Even if you're not an avid angler, the complimentary fishing experience is a fun diversion. Fishing rods and bait are provided free of charge, so you don't need to bring anything. The crew makes it easy and beginner-friendly.

You might catch local Jeju species such as rockfish (우럭), scorpionfish (쏨뱅이), filefish (쥐치), or even a puffer fish (복어). It's a great way to engage with the natural environment. And don't worry, it's catch-and-release, so you're not obligated to keep your prizes. It’s all about the experience and a good laugh.
