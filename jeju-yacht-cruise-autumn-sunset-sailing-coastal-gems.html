What to Expect: Your 60-Minute Coastal Journey

Each 60-minute Jeju yacht cruise departs from Daepo Port in Seogwipo, taking you past some of Jeju Island's most breathtaking geological formations. This is where the landscape tells a story of volcanic creation, a UNESCO-style geological wonder.

The route includes iconic spots like the Elephant Rock (Kkokkiribawi), a natural cliff aptly shaped like its namesake. You’ll also glide past the dramatic Jingotnae Sea Canyon and the unique hexagonal columns of Wolpyeong Basalt Columns (Wolpyeong Jusangjeolli). Keep an eye out for the mysterious Wolpyeong Sea Cave and the expansive Pangpaengideok reef plateau, perfect for photography.

There's even a Large Sea Cave (Keunbada Donggul) along the route, adding a touch of adventure to your scenic tour. For those booking a Jeju autumn sunset cruise, the entire coastline becomes a canvas of gold and crimson, offering truly spectacular 360-degree panoramic views only available once a day.

Cast a Line: Fishing Fun for Everyone

Want to try your hand at fishing without the hassle of bringing your own gear? Every Grande Bleu Jeju sailing tour includes a complimentary deck fishing experience. Whether you’re a seasoned angler or a complete novice, the crew provides all the fishing rods and bait you need, absolutely free.

You might reel in local Jeju species like rockfish (우럭), scorpionfish (쏨뱅이), filefish (쥐치), or even a puffer fish (복어). It's a fun, family-friendly activity, and catch-and-release is always an option. This added element makes the Jeju catamaran cruise more interactive and memorable, fulfilling that desire for unique activities during your Korea trip.
