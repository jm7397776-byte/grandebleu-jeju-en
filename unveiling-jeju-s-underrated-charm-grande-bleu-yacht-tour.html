What to Expect: Your 60-Minute Grande Bleu Jeju Catamaran Cruise

The 60-minute Grande Bleu yacht tour from Daepo Port in Seogwipo is more than just a ride; it's a coastal discovery of Jeju's unique geology and marine life. This route offers a slow, immersive experience of Jeju's rugged beauty, a truly underrated highlight of South Korea. You'll glide past a series of natural wonders that are best appreciated from the ocean.

As you sail, keep an eye out for these iconic sights:

  • Elephant Rock (Kkokkiribawi): A fascinating cliff formation that truly resembles an elephant's trunk dipping into the sea.
  • Jingotnae Sea Canyon: Deep, dramatic cliffs with vertical walls carved by nature.
  • Wolpyeong Basalt Columns (Wolpyeong Jusangjeolli): Hexagonal volcanic sea cliffs, part of Jeju's UNESCO Global Geopark. These geological formations are a sight to behold, reminiscent of Giant's Causeway or other world-renowned basalt features.
  • Wolpyeong Sea Cave: A mysterious sea cave where waves crash against ancient rock.
  • Pangpaengideok: A wide, flat reef plateau offering unique photo opportunities.
  • Large Sea Cave (Keunbada Donggul): The biggest sea cave along this stretch of coast.

The professional crew onboard are also photography enthusiasts. They are eager to help you capture those perfect anniversary shots, often anticipating great photo spots like the Wolpyeong Basalt Columns. Many guests rave about how friendly and proactive the staff are in offering to take pictures, ensuring you both get in the frame.
