The Coastal Route: Actually Worth Photographing

The 60-minute loop from Daepo Port covers a stretch of the Jeju coast you cannot see from land. Highlights:

  • Elephant Rock (Kkokkiribawi) — an obvious elephant silhouette carved by waves
  • Wolpyeong Basalt Columns — hexagonal volcanic cliffs, part of Jeju's UNESCO Geopark geology
  • Jingotnae Sea Canyon — vertical cliff walls that feel oddly Norwegian
  • Wolpyeong Sea Cave and the larger Keunbada Donggul
  • Pangpaengideok, a flat reef plateau that photographs brilliantly at golden hour

October light in Jeju is soft and long. The Jeju autumn sunset hits differently than summer — cleaner air, less haze, more colour in the sky. If photography is your thing, autumn is arguably the best window.

Fishing, Dolphins, and Actually Doing Something

You can fish off the deck. Rods and bait are included, and you'll likely pull up rockfish, scorpionfish, filefish or the occasional puffer. Catch-and-release if you're not into keeping them.

If you're lucky, you'll spot Jeju's resident bottlenose dolphins — the boat passes through their known habitat. No guarantee, but sightings are common enough that the crew doesn't oversell it.

For Jeju family activities, this ticks a lot of boxes: kids fish, grandparents sit comfortably on the shaded deck, everyone eats. No one's stuck watching a screen.

Practical Tips for October Sailing

  • Book the last departure of the day — the golden hour happens once, don't miss it
  • Bring a light jacket; Jeju sea breeze in autumn bites after sunset
  • Daepo Port is 15 minutes from Jungmun resort area, 40 minutes from Jeju City
  • Weekdays are noticeably quieter than weekends
  • Motion sickness pills aren't really needed on the catamaran, but bring one if you're anxious

Jeju sailing in October generally means calm seas and clear skies — statistically the best weather window of the year.
