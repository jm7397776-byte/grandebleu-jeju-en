Coastal Wonders & Golden Hour Magic

The 60-minute route takes you along the stunning Seogwipo coast, revealing geological marvels and hidden coves. You'll glide past:

  • Elephant Rock (Kkokkiribawi): A natural cliff shaped uncannily like an elephant.
  • Jingotnae Sea Canyon: Deep cliff canyon with dramatic vertical walls.
  • Wolpyeong Basalt Columns (Wolpyeong Jusangjeolli): Unique hexagonal volcanic sea cliffs, part of Jeju’s UNESCO Global Geopark.
  • Wolpyeong Sea Cave: A mysterious sea cave where waves crash against ancient formations.
  • Pangpaengideok: A broad, photogenic sea-level rock platform.
  • Large Sea Cave (Keunbada Donggul): The biggest sea cave along this stretch of coast.

While a guaranteed wild dolphin sighting isn't possible (they are, after all, wild animals), you'll pass through known habitat zones for the Indo-Pacific bottlenose dolphin, offering a chance to spot these magnificent creatures.

For many, the Jeju sunset cruise is the pinnacle of the experience. As the sun dips below the horizon, the sky and sea transform into a canvas of golden hues, offering truly 360-degree panoramic views. This one-hour window of pure magic is undoubtedly the most popular time slot, and honestly, it’s an utterly captivating experience that often evokes "wow" moments from even the most jaded travellers. It’s an incredible photo opportunity, but more importantly, a moment to simply soak in the tranquil beauty of Jeju.
