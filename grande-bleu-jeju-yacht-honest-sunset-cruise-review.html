If you're flying into Jeju for a business trip this winter and have a half-day window, this is the kind of low-effort, high-payoff outing that earns its place on the itinerary — without leaning on the usual "breathtaking" hyperbole.

Why a Catamaran, Not a Tour Boat

Most Jeju "yacht tours" are repurposed sightseeing boats. Grande Bleu is the only certified catamaran brand in South Korea — twin-hull, wider deck, noticeably steadier in winter chop than a monohull.

That matters in December through February, when the Korea Strait gets brisk. You can stand on the bow without bracing yourself, which is the difference between a real photo and a blurry one.

It's also why the Shilla Hotel and Parnas Jeju route their guests here. Five-star partnerships aren't decoration; they reflect the operational baseline.

The Route: 60 Minutes Along Seogwipo's South Coast

The course follows Seogwipo's volcanic shoreline — a stretch that's part of Jeju's UNESCO Global Geopark designation. You're not circling open water; you're tracing cliffs, sea caves, and basalt formations the entire hour.

What you'll pass:

  • Wolpyeong Basalt Columns — hexagonal volcanic sea cliffs, the same geology UNESCO recognised
  • Elephant Rock (Kkokkiribawi) — a cliff that genuinely looks like an elephant from the water
  • Jingotnae Sea Canyon — vertical cliff walls dropping straight into the sea
  • Wolpyeong Sea Cave and the larger Keunbada Donggul — the biggest sea cave on the coast
  • Pangpaengideok — a wide, flat reef plateau that photographs unreasonably well

The sunset slot runs once a day, timed to golden hour. In winter that lands around 17:30, which works neatly after a half-day of meetings.

Food and Drinks: Actually Unlimited

This is where Jeju catamaran tours diverge sharply, and where Grande Bleu is genuinely generous. Everything onboard is free and refills are unlimited — no card-tap moment halfway through.

What's poured and served:

  • Korean draft beer (fresh brew)
  • Red and white wine
  • Fresh-squeezed Jeju tangerine juice
  • Bottled water
  • Local Jeju snacks and traditional cookies
  • Korean ramyeon, cooked onboard

For a solo traveller on a business trip, the practical read is: this replaces dinner. A glass of wine on the bow at sunset, a hot bowl of ramyeon as the sky turns, and you've handled both your evening meal and your one cultural moment for the day.

Fishing — Included, Not Upsold

Rods and bait are provided at no extra cost. You're targeting real Jeju species: rockfish, scorpionfish, filefish, sometimes pufferfish. Catch-and-release is standard.

It's beginner-friendly and a low-pressure way to fill the middle stretch of the cruise. Skip it if you'd rather photograph the coast — nobody pushes.

Sunset vs Luxury (Daytime): Which to Pick

Two slots run daily, same route, same vessel, same inclusions.

  • Luxury (daytime): Adult ₩60,000 / Child ₩40,000 — sharper colour on the basalt, clearer water reading
  • Sunset: Adult ₩70,000 / Child ₩40,000 — golden hour panorama, runs once daily

If you're here in winter and chasing the warm-weather feel, the daytime sail actually delivers more sun on deck. The sunset slot is the photographer's choice. Both finish in 60 minutes, so there's no logistics penalty either way.

MICE and corporate group rates are available for Jeju team building bookings — useful if you're scoping this for a company offsite rather than a solo run.

Honest Trade-offs

A few things worth knowing before you book:

  • It's one hour, not a half-day cruise. If you want extended sailing, this isn't it.
  • The deck is exposed. In January, bring a windproof layer — the cabin is heated but the views aren't from inside.
  • Phone support is Korean-only (+82-64-739-7776). Book through an English-language platform and you won't need to call.

None of these are dealbreakers. They're just the reasons this gets a four-star, not a fluffed-up five.

FAQ

How long is the Jeju sunset cruise? The Grande Bleu sunset cruise runs 60 minutes from Daepo Port. It's timed daily to golden hour, which falls around 17:30 in winter and later in summer.

Is food included on the Jeju yacht? Yes — draft beer, red and white wine, Jeju tangerine juice, water, snacks, and freshly cooked Korean ramyeon are all included at no extra charge, with unlimited refills.

Where does the Jeju yacht depart from? All cruises depart from Daepo Port in Seogwipo on Jeju Island's south coast (GPS 33.2287°N 126.4318°E). It's roughly 40 minutes by car from Jeju International Airport.

What's the best time for a sunset cruise in Jeju? Winter sunsets (December–February) give the warmest colour palette and clearest air. The single daily sunset departure fills up faster than the daytime slot, so book a few days ahead.
