Unveiling Jeju's Coastal Secrets: The 60-Minute Tour Route

The Grande Bleu Jeju catamaran tour isn't just about sailing; it’s a guided journey along Seogwipo Jeju's most picturesque coastline. Departing from Daepo Port, you'll get up close to natural formations that are often missed from land.

  • Coastal Highlights:
  • Elephant Rock (Kkokkiribawi): A remarkable natural cliff shaped uncannily like an elephant.
  • Jingotnae Sea Canyon: A deep, dramatic cliff canyon with impressive vertical walls.
  • Wolpyeong Basalt Columns (Wolpyeong Jusangjeolli): Hexagonal volcanic sea cliffs, part of Jeju's UNESCO Global Geopark. Seeing these columnar joints from the sea offers a truly grand perspective.
  • Wolpyeong Sea Cave: A mysterious sea cave where waves crash dramatically.
  • Pangpaengideok: A broad, flat reef plateau, perfect for capturing some unique photos.
  • Large Sea Cave (Keunbada Donggul): The biggest sea cave along this stretch of coast.

_The unique Elephant Rock as seen from the Grande Bleu yacht._

The 60-minute route is carefully chosen to showcase these stunning geological wonders, offering unique photo opportunities, especially during the 360-degree panoramic golden hour views of the sunset cruise. While not guaranteed (they are wild, after all!), it adds an extra layer of excitement to your Jeju sunset cruise.
