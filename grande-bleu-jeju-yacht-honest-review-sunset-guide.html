Why a Catamaran Matters (More Than You'd Think)

Most yacht tours in Jeju use single-hull boats. They roll. If you're sensitive to motion — and many travellers in their 60s are — that matters.

A catamaran has two hulls, which disperses roughly 90% of wave force. The deck stays flatter, the horizon stops swaying, and you can actually hold a glass of wine without bracing yourself. For a family vacation with grandkids or a quiet trip for two, that stability is the whole game.

The teak deck is another quiet detail. Real wood underfoot, not painted fibreglass. It's the kind of finish you'd expect on a private charter in the Mediterranean — and it's part of why Grande Bleu compares more naturally to a small Aman cruise than a typical Jeju activity.

The 60-Minute Coastal Route

Daepo Port sits on Seogwipo's south coast, one minute from the coast guard station — a practical safety detail rather than a marketing line. Average wave height in these waters runs around 37 cm, calm by any standard.

Within an hour you cover:

  • Wolpyeong Basalt Columns — hexagonal volcanic cliffs (UNESCO Geopark geology), visible only from the sea at this angle
  • Wolpyeong Sea Cave — waves echoing off basalt
  • Elephant Rock (Kkokkiribawi) — silhouette shifts with the light
  • Jingotnae Sea Canyon — vertical cliff walls dropping into deep water
  • Pangpaengideok — a wide flat reef plateau, the most photogenic stretch
  • Large Sea Cave (Keunbada Donggul) — the biggest on the coast

If you've done coastal cruises in Bali or the Greek islands, the scale here is smaller but the geology is more dramatic. Jeju is volcanic, and the basalt columns rival anything you'll see in Iceland.

Fishing — Optional and Low-Key

Rods and bait are provided. Local catches include rockfish, scorpionfish, filefish, and pufferfish. Catch-and-release is fine.

Don't book this expecting a fishing trip. It's a five-minute pause where the engines drop, the boat sits still, and you get a closer feel for the water. Kids and grandkids tend to remember this part most.

The Sunset Window

Jeju summer evenings (June, July, August) hold light late — sunset lands roughly 19:30–19:45. The golden hour onboard is genuinely 360-degrees because the boat rotates through the route. Once a day, once an hour.

For photos, the last 20 minutes before docking are the strongest. Soft side-light on the teak deck, basalt cliffs in warm tones, the horizon clean.
