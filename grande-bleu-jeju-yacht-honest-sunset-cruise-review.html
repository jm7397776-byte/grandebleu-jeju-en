Why Daepo Port, Not Seongsan

Seongsan is the sunrise side of Jeju — the name "Seongsan Ilchulbong" literally means Sunrise Peak. Beautiful in the morning, less interesting at golden hour.

Daepo, on the south coast near Jungmun, faces west. When the sun drops toward the horizon, you don't need to reposition the boat or fight for the right side of the deck. The light comes to you.

Three practical reasons Daepo works better for sunset:

  • Orientation: West-facing route — the horizon opens up the moment you clear the harbour
  • Coastline: Volcanic cliffs, sea caves and basalt columns change colour as the light shifts
  • Sea conditions: Mt. Hallasan blocks the north wind, so the south coast stays calmer — steady enough to hold a beer on deck

If you've done the Bali sunset booze cruise or the Santorini catamaran circuit, this is the quieter cousin. Fewer boats, weirder geology, colder beer.

The 60-Minute Route (And Why It's Actually Worth It)

Most Jeju boat tours give you open water and a horizon. Grande Bleu's route hugs the coast, which means something to look at the whole hour.

  • Elephant Rock (Kkokkiribawi) — a cliff that genuinely looks like an elephant lowering its trunk
  • Jingotnae Sea Canyon — vertical cliffs dropping straight into the sea
  • Wolpyeong Basalt Columns — hexagonal volcanic pillars, part of Jeju's UNESCO Global Geopark
  • Wolpyeong Sea Cave and the Large Sea Cave (Keunbada Donggul) — only visible from the water
  • Pangpaengideok — a wide flat reef plateau, the photo everyone takes

You can hike to similar basalt columns on land at Daepo Jusangjeolli, but you only see them straight-on from the sea. That's the actual ROI here.

Fishing — Yes, Real Fishing

Rods and bait are included. You're fishing for actual Jeju species: rockfish (uruk), scorpionfish (ssombaeng-i), filefish (jwichi), and puffer (bok-eo). Catch-and-release is the default for safety. Total beginner-friendly — kids and first-timers land fish most days.

Winter in Jeju: Why December–February Still Works

Jeju winter is not Bali winter, but it's not Seoul winter either. Daytime highs sit around 8–12°C, the south coast is shielded from the north wind, and sunset hits earlier (around 17:30) — meaning you can do the cruise and still make a 19:00 dinner in Jungmun.

For SEA travellers used to year-round heat, pack a windproof layer and you'll be fine on deck. The crew hands out blankets.
