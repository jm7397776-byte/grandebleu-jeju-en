Why a Catamaran Actually Matters

Most Jeju boat tours run single-hull vessels. They roll. Jeju's average coastal swell is only around 37cm, which sounds gentle until you've been on a mono-hull for forty minutes holding a glass of wine you can't actually drink.

A catamaran absorbs roughly 90% of that lateral motion through its twin hulls. In practice: you can stand on the deck, pour a glass, and not spill it. That's the entire pitch.

For a couple in their fifties — or anyone who's previously written off boat trips because of motion sickness — this is the structural reason Grande Bleu gets repeat bookings rather than one-and-done tourists.

It's also why the operator holds Korea's only certified catamaran brand status and partners with the Shilla Hotel and Parnas Jeju for guest excursions. Hotels at that tier don't recommend tours that make their guests queasy.

The Coastal Route in 60 Minutes

The boat leaves Daepo Port and tracks west along Seogwipo's volcanic coastline. You don't see this geology from land — the cliffs face the sea, so the only honest view is from the water.

What you'll pass:

  • Wolpyeong Basalt Columns — hexagonal volcanic cliffs (same UNESCO Geopark-grade geology as Jusangjeolli)
  • Wolpyeong Sea Cave — waves crashing into a black volcanic mouth
  • Elephant Rock (Kkokkiribawi) — a natural cliff that genuinely looks like one
  • Jingotnae Sea Canyon — vertical walls dropping into deep water
  • Pangpaengideok — a wide flat reef platform that photographs better than it sounds
  • Keunbada Donggul — the largest sea cave on this stretch of coast

Mid-route the engine cuts and the fishing rods come out. Real Jeju species — rockfish, scorpionfish, filefish, sometimes puffer. Bait and gear are free, catch-and-release if you prefer.

Jeju in October: Why Autumn Is the Quiet Win

Summer Jeju is humid, crowded, and the light is harsh. Winter is cold and the sea gets moody. Autumn — September through November — is the season locals quietly recommend.

The air drops to a comfortable 18–22°C, the haze burns off, and the sun sets between 5:30 and 6:15pm. Translation: you board around 5pm, you're holding a glass on deck by 5:20pm, and the entire return leg is bathed in low gold light.

For photography this matters. Autumn Jeju gives you that warm horizontal light that flatters faces and turns the basalt cliffs amber. The Instagram crowd will tell you it's the best season; they're right for once.
